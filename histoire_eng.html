Paris Architecture

A journey through styles and centuries

Introduction

Paris is a true open-air museum. At every street corner, the architecture tells the story of the city, its artistic, social, and political revolutions. From medieval remains to the bold lines of contemporary architecture, the French capital is a mosaic of styles.

Middle Ages (4th - 15th centuries)
The Foundations of Paris
Medieval architecture in Paris is characterized by its Gothic churches and early fortifications.
  • 4th - 10th century: Basilica of Saints-Andrés, Saint-Germain-des-Prés Abbey
  • 1163: Beginning of Notre-Dame de Paris (Gothic)
  • Philip Augustus: Enclosure and Louvre Castle
  • Charles V: Enclosure with Bastille, modernization of the Palais de la Cité
  • 15th century: Hôtel de Cluny, Saint-Merri Church (Flamboyant Gothic)
Modern Era (16th - 18th century)
Renaissance and Classicism
The Renaissance brought new architectural codes, followed by the French classical age.
  • 16th century: Classical/Baroque style - Place des Vosges, Place Vendôme
  • 17th century: Neoclassicism - Panthéon
  • 18th century: Haussmann's works - grand boulevards, stone buildings
Antiquity (1st - 3rd century)
Lutetia, Ancient Paris
The Gallo-Roman remains testify to the origins of the city.
  • Lutetia (ancient Paris) on the Île de la Cité and left bank
  • Thermes de Cluny (Roman forum)
  • Arènes de Lutèce (amphitheater)
Renaissance and Modern Times (15th - 18th century)
Architectural Evolution
Introduction of the Italian Renaissance and development of French classical style.
  • Fontaine des Innocents (Jean Goujon), Louvre (Pierre Lescot)
  • Private mansions between courtyard and garden (e.g., Hôtel Carnavalet)
  • Churches: Saint-Étienne-du-Mont, Saint-Gervais-Saint-Protais
20th - 21st centuries
Modernity and Innovation
Contemporary Parisian architecture between heritage and innovation.
  • 20th century: Modernity - Centre Pompidou, Montparnasse Tower, La Défense
  • 21st century: Contemporary architecture, balance between heritage and innovation
