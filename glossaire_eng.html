Banks

The banks refer to the shores of the river, often developed for walking, cultural or sporting activities. In Paris, some banks of the Seine are pedestrianized, offering relaxation spaces for users as well as impressions of the city. Since 2016, the right bank has been transformed into a pedestrian zone, promoting cultural and leisure activities.

Quay

The term "quay" refers to a road running along a waterway. In Paris, the quays run along the Seine and often bear names of historical accompaniments or significant events. Bateaux-Mouches

Tourist boats offering cruises on the Seine to discover Paris from the river. These boats offer a unique perspective on the city's iconic monuments, such as Notre-Dame Cathedral, the Louvre and the Eiffel Tower.

Bateaux-Mouches

Barge

Flat-bottomed boat formerly used for transporting goods. Today, many barges in Paris are converted into housing, restaurants or cultural venues, contributing to the charm and diversity of the quays.

Barge

Bridge

Structure allowing to cross the Seine. Paris has 37 bridges crossing the river, each with its own history and distinctive architecture. Among the most famous are the Pont Neuf, the oldest in the city, and the Pont Alexandre III, renowned for its ornamental richness.

Bridge

Port

Part of the quay designed to accommodate boats. In Paris, certain ports, such as the Port de l'Arsenal, have become lively living spaces, welcoming pleasure boats and offering green spaces for walkers.

Georges Pompidou Way

Former expressway running along the Seine, right bank, in central Paris, discovered through pedestrianization with leisure spaces, known as "Seine Banks". This transformation has made it possible to give Parisians direct access to the river and to create new relaxation spaces in the heart of the city.

Bouquinistes

Sellers of second-hand books and old documents installed in green boxes along the quays. About 250 in number. They represent a unique tradition in the world, active for several centuries, and inscribed on UNESCO's intangible cultural heritage.

UNESCO

UNESCO and Heritage

Urban Planning

Work aimed at rehabilitating the quays to promote soft mobility, greenery and public access to the river. These developments have made it possible to transform former industrial or road zones into promenade, cultural and leisure spaces, contributing to improving the quality of life of Parisians and visitors.

Waterfront Roads

Former roads located closest to the river, some have been pedestrianized to create cultural and relaxation spaces. This initiative has made it possible to reduce pollution, encourage travel on foot or by bicycle, and give Parisians privileged access to the banks of the Seine.

Urban Planning

Upper Quays / Lower Quays

  • Upper quays: elevated, constituting the main roads running along the Seine.
  • Lower quays: closer to the river, accessible for walking or cultural events.
